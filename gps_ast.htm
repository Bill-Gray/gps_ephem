Check GNSS astrometry

Click here for an explanation of what this page is and why it exists.

Cut and paste astrometry in the MPC's 80-column MPC format (ADES to follow), and click 'Process observations'. The program will figure out which GNSS satellite(s) you observed and tell you how far off you were in along-track seconds and cross-track arcseconds, for each observation. (The object designations can be anything you like; they'll be ignored.) At the bottom, you'll get averages and standard deviations.

There are several other tools for asteroid observers on this site, including a tool to list GNSS satellites visible from a given site.
